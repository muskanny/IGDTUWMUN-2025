UNHRC

The United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC) is an esteemed international body committed to safeguarding and promoting human rights worldwide. With 47 member states, it addresses critical issues such as discrimination, inequality, and violations of fundamental freedoms. The UNHRC aims to uphold justice and equality for all through dialogue, cooperation, and actionable solutions. At Model United Nations, this committee empowers delegates to engage in meaningful debates, draft resolutions, and champion global efforts towards a fair and inclusive world.




The agenda of the United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC) is to prevent the widespread increase of xenophobia and ethnocentric discrimination with special emphasis on Sustainable Development Goal 10 (SDG 10).



About the agenda - Xenophobia's expressions range from discriminatory attitudes and remarks to institutional or social exclusion, harassment and overt forms of interpersonal and collective violence. UN Human Rights provides extensive support to all the mechanisms involved in the UN fight against racism, and works with them to make the Durban Declaration and Programme of Action a reality.
